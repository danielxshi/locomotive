Research

Interviewing the Experts

We started with several rounds of interviews with the Moment Team to establish the design space. Then, we got in contact with potential clients to understand their wants, needs, pains, and motivations. Due to the Coronavirus pandemic, this process was done exclusively via remote conferencing.

Ethnography

Moment Energy is a local clean-tech startup that repurposes used automotive lithium batteries into battery storage solutions for individuals and businesses. They strive to supply their clients with efficient and clean energy storage solutions that are competitive on the market.

Interviews

Sketch

After a difficult start to our process, we finally were presented with an opportunity to speak to potential end users of Moment Energy’s product. Given that our participant organization only had one fully established client, it was very lucky for us to get this opportunity. We combined the information we learned from Moment Energy directly with the new found information we gathered from solar power integrators and owners to produce a more specific and powerful focus.

Persona

After speaking with end users, we established a clear target audience: the clean energy enthusiast. This person is innately interested in clean energy, hence why they are in the market for clean energy tech.

Journey Map

They are goal-oriented and conscious about money, often needing to balance the two. They are very willing to work with smaller companies that have promising innovations and noble aspirations, so long as it makes sense financially. They are willing to take the risk, but need extensive proof that the product is as good as the company says it is.

Participatory Workshop

Findings

Through 12 weeks of research and multiple pivots, we identified both Moment and their customers value their relationship built upon trust. We found alignment to create a user experience for Moment’s customers to further develop their relationship with Moment.

Design

The Design

Insecurity from lack of information, presents itself as reluctance to use and trust the battery. We developed an application called Moment Track to provide a user interface for customers to keep updated of their product and ultimately provide the extra layer of reliability and trust for Moment’s product.
